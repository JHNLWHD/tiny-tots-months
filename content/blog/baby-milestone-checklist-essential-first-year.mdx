# Baby Milestone Checklist: Essential First-Year Achievements

Your baby's first year is filled with incredible milestones. This comprehensive checklist helps you track the essential achievements across all areas of development. Use it as a guide to celebrate progress and identify any areas where your baby might need extra support.

## How to Use This Checklist

- **Check off milestones** as your baby achieves them
- **Note the date** when each milestone occurs
- **Don't stress** if your baby reaches milestones at different times
- **Focus on progress** rather than exact timing
- **Consult your pediatrician** if you have concerns

Remember: Every baby develops at their own pace. This checklist shows typical ranges, but variations are completely normal!

## 0-3 Months Milestones

### Physical Development
- [ ] Lifts head briefly when on tummy
- [ ] Turns head from side to side
- [ ] Opens and closes hands
- [ ] Brings hands to mouth
- [ ] Kicks legs and moves arms
- [ ] Pushes up on arms during tummy time
- [ ] Holds head steady when upright (by 3 months)

### Language Development
- [ ] Makes cooing sounds
- [ ] Cries differently for different needs
- [ ] Responds to sounds by turning head
- [ ] Recognizes your voice
- [ ] Makes vowel-like sounds ("ah," "eh," "oh")

### Social & Emotional Development
- [ ] Makes eye contact
- [ ] First social smile (usually 6-8 weeks)
- [ ] Recognizes familiar faces
- [ ] Responds to your touch
- [ ] Shows interest in faces

### Cognitive Development
- [ ] Follows objects with eyes
- [ ] Recognizes familiar objects
- [ ] Shows interest in environment
- [ ] Responds to voices and sounds

## 3-6 Months Milestones

### Physical Development
- [ ] Rolls from tummy to back
- [ ] Sits with support
- [ ] Holds head steady without support
- [ ] Reaches for objects
- [ ] Grasps and shakes toys
- [ ] Brings objects to mouth
- [ ] Bears weight on legs when held

### Language Development
- [ ] Babbles with consonants ("ba," "ma," "da")
- [ ] Laughs and squeals
- [ ] Responds to name
- [ ] Makes different sounds for different emotions
- [ ] Imitates some sounds

### Social & Emotional Development
- [ ] Enjoys playing with others
- [ ] Shows excitement when seeing familiar people
- [ ] Responds to emotions in your voice
- [ ] Shows interest in mirror
- [ ] May show stranger awareness

### Cognitive Development
- [ ] Explores objects with hands and mouth
- [ ] Shows interest in cause and effect
- [ ] Recognizes familiar objects and people
- [ ] Follows moving objects with eyes
- [ ] Understands object permanence begins (things exist when out of sight)

## 6-9 Months Milestones

### Physical Development
- [ ] Sits without support
- [ ] Rolls both ways (tummy to back and back to tummy)
- [ ] Crawls or scoots
- [ ] Transfers objects between hands
- [ ] Uses raking grasp
- [ ] Bangs objects together
- [ ] Pulls to standing with support

### Language Development
- [ ] Reduplicated babbling ("mama," "dada," "baba")
- [ ] Responds to "no"
- [ ] Understands tone of voice
- [ ] Makes sounds to get attention
- [ ] Imitates sounds and gestures

### Social & Emotional Development
- [ ] Shows separation anxiety
- [ ] May show stranger anxiety
- [ ] Plays peek-a-boo
- [ ] Shows preferences for people
- [ ] Enjoys interactive games

### Cognitive Development
- [ ] Understands object permanence
- [ ] Explores cause and effect
- [ ] Problem solves (getting toys from containers)
- [ ] Imitates actions
- [ ] Shows curiosity about environment

## 9-12 Months Milestones

### Physical Development
- [ ] Stands holding furniture
- [ ] Cruises along furniture
- [ ] May take first steps
- [ ] Pincer grasp (thumb and finger)
- [ ] Puts objects in containers
- [ ] Stacks 2-3 blocks
- [ ] May walk independently (some babies)

### Language Development
- [ ] Says first words (usually "mama," "dada," "hi," "bye")
- [ ] Understands simple commands
- [ ] Points to request things
- [ ] Waves bye-bye
- [ ] Says 1-3 words by 12 months
- [ ] Understands 50+ words

### Social & Emotional Development
- [ ] Shows attachment to primary caregivers
- [ ] Plays interactive games (pat-a-cake)
- [ ] Shows preferences for toys and activities
- [ ] Shows sense of humor
- [ ] May show empathy

### Cognitive Development
- [ ] Follows simple directions
- [ ] Points to body parts when named
- [ ] Understands "give me"
- [ ] Shows problem-solving skills
- [ ] Imitates actions and sounds

## Month-by-Month Quick Reference

### Month 1
- Social smile, head control, cooing

### Month 2
- More alert, follows objects, varied sounds

### Month 3
- Holds head steady, reaches for objects, laughs

### Month 4
- Rolls tummy to back, sits with support, advanced babbling

### Month 5
- Sits with less support, explores objects, shows preferences

### Month 6
- Sits without support, says first words (some), starts solids

### Month 7
- Crawls, understands "no," shows attachment

### Month 8
- Pulls to standing, says "mama"/"dada" (may not be specific), waves

### Month 9
- Cruises, points, understands simple commands

### Month 10
- Stands alone briefly, says 1-2 words, follows one-step directions

### Month 11
- Takes first steps (some), says 2-3 words, shows more independence

### Month 12
- Walks (some), says 2-6 words, follows two-step commands, first birthday!

## Understanding Variations

### What's Normal?

- **Wide ranges**: Milestones have wide normal ranges
- **Different paces**: Babies develop at different speeds
- **Domain differences**: May be ahead in one area, behind in another
- **Temporary delays**: Some babies pause, then catch up

### When to Consult Your Pediatrician

While variations are normal, consult your pediatrician if:

**By 3 months:**
- No social smile
- Can't hold head up
- Not responding to sounds

**By 6 months:**
- Not rolling over
- Not reaching for objects
- No babbling

**By 9 months:**
- Not sitting without support
- Not showing interest in environment
- No babbling with consonants

**By 12 months:**
- Not crawling or showing mobility
- No words
- Not following simple commands
- Not pointing

## Tips for Using This Checklist

### 1. Don't Stress About Timing

Focus on progress, not exact dates. Your baby will reach milestones in their own time.

### 2. Celebrate All Achievements

Every milestone, whether early or late, is worth celebrating!

### 3. Look for Patterns

Notice if your baby is consistently ahead or behind in certain areas. This can help identify strengths and areas for support.

### 4. Use It as a Guide

This checklist is a guide, not a test. Use it to understand typical development, not to compare your baby to others.

### 5. Document with Photos

Take photos and videos of milestones. You'll treasure these memories!

## Supporting Development

### Physical Milestones
- Provide plenty of tummy time
- Create safe exploration spaces
- Offer age-appropriate toys
- Encourage movement and play

### Language Milestones
- Talk to your baby constantly
- Read books daily
- Sing songs and rhymes
- Respond to all communication attempts

### Social Milestones
- Provide consistent care
- Play interactive games
- Arrange social opportunities
- Respond to emotions

### Cognitive Milestones
- Provide stimulating environment
- Offer cause-and-effect toys
- Play problem-solving games
- Follow your baby's interests
